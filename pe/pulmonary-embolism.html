Pulmonary Embolism on CTPA

Acute vs chronic clot, right-heart strain, and the saddle. Copy-ready findings/impressions — replace [bracketed] text.

🚨 Report RV strain on every positive PE. A saddle or large central clot with RV:LV ratio > 1.0, septal bowing, and reflux of contrast into the IVC/hepatic veins flags a potentially unstable patient — communicate it.

Protocol & Quality

Getting a diagnostic CTPA

🧪ProtocolBolus timing is everything
  • Pulmonary arterial phase: bolus-track on the main PA (target ≥ ~250 HU). Suboptimal opacification is the main cause of a non-diagnostic study.
  • Transient interruption of contrast (deep inspiration draws unopacified IVC blood into the right heart) can mimic central clot — look for a flow-related gradient, not a discrete filling defect.
  • Read on a PE/narrow window; scroll, don't snapshot — subsegmental clots hide at the periphery.

Diagnostic Findings

Acute PE

🔍How to find itIntraluminal filling defect
  • Central defect surrounded by contrast (polo-mint sign in cross-section) or a partial filling defect at an acute angle to the wall (railway-track sign longitudinally).
  • Acute clot expands the vessel and sits centrally; saddle embolus straddles the main PA bifurcation.
  • Map the most proximal extent: main / lobar / segmental / subsegmental.

Right-heart strain — measure it

📏ThresholdSigns of RV strain
  • RV:LV short-axis diameter > 1.0 on axial (best simple marker of strain).
  • Interventricular septal bowing toward the LV (straightening/leftward).
  • Reflux of contrast into the IVC and hepatic veins (elevated right-heart pressure).
  • Main PA diameter > ~29 mm suggests pulmonary hypertension.
💡PearlPulmonary infarct

Peripheral wedge-shaped consolidation abutting the pleura with central lucencies (Hampton hump) supports infarction from a distal embolus; check for a feeding vessel sign.

I: Acute [segmental] pulmonary embolism without CT evidence of right-heart strain.

Negative CTPA

F: Diagnostic-quality CT pulmonary angiogram with adequate opacification to the subsegmental level. No filling defect in the main, lobar, segmental, or subsegmental pulmonary arteries. Normal RV:LV ratio. No pericardial effusion.

I: No pulmonary embolism. [Incidental findings as below.]

Search pattern

  • 1. Confirm opacification adequacy; rule out flow artifact / transient interruption.
  • 2. Track main → lobar → segmental → subsegmental arteries on the PE window.
  • 3. Measure RV:LV; assess septum and IVC reflux.
  • 4. Lung windows: infarct, pneumonia, alternative cause of dyspnea/pain.
  • 5. Communicate central/saddle PE or RV strain.
