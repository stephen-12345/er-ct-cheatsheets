Acute Mesenteric Ischemia on CT

A high-mortality miss with a subtle early CT. Pain out of proportion to exam — look hard at the vessels and the bowel wall. Replace [bracketed] text.

🚨 Pain out of proportion = mesenteric ischemia until excluded. Early CT can look near-normal. Scrutinize the SMA/SMV, bowel-wall enhancement, and mesentery on the arterial + venous phases.

Acute SMA occlusion with ischemia

F: Filling defect occluding the [proximal/mid] superior mesenteric artery with non-enhancement of [a segment of jejunum/ileum], which is dilated and fluid-filled with a thin, poorly enhancing wall. Mesenteric stranding and free fluid. [Pneumatosis / portal venous gas.]

I: Acute superior mesenteric artery occlusion with bowel ischemia [and signs of necrosis] — surgical emergency. Communicated to [name] at [time].

SMV thrombosis

F: Expansile filling defect within the superior mesenteric vein with surrounding mesenteric edema and engorged collaterals. Segmental small-bowel wall thickening with submucosal edema and mucosal hyperenhancement. No pneumatosis or free air.

I: Superior mesenteric vein thrombosis with congestive small-bowel ischemia. [Recommend anticoagulation; evaluate for hypercoagulable state.]

Negative

F: Patent SMA, SMV, celiac axis, and IMA. Normal bowel-wall enhancement and caliber without pneumatosis, mesenteric edema, or portal venous gas.

I: No CT evidence of mesenteric ischemia.

Search pattern

  • 1. Trace SMA and celiac (arterial phase) and SMV/portal (venous phase) for occlusion/thrombus.
  • 2. Assess bowel-wall enhancement (hypo vs hyper), caliber, and the mesentery.
  • 3. Hunt pneumatosis and portal venous gas (lung/wide windows).
  • 4. Distinguish mechanism (embolic / thrombotic / venous / NOMI).
  • 5. Communicate — outcomes are time-dependent.

Mechanisms

Four mechanisms — each looks different

TypeKey CT
Arterial embolism (SMA)Abrupt filling defect, often a few cm from the origin (sparing proximal jejunum); cardiac source
Arterial thrombosisOcclusion at a heavily atherosclerotic/calcified SMA origin
Venous thrombosis (SMV)Expanded SMV with central filling defect, mesenteric edema/fluid, bowel-wall thickening
Non-occlusive (NOMI)Patent vessels but low-flow state; diffuse poor enhancement, vasospasm; ICU/shock patient

Bowel-Wall Findings

What the wall tells you

🔍How to find itTwo opposite enhancement patterns
  • Hypoenhancement / thin wall ("paper-thin"): arterial occlusion — the most specific early sign.
  • Hyperenhancement / thick wall with edema: venous occlusion or reperfusion.
  • Loss of normal mucosal enhancement, dilatation with fluid, mesenteric stranding/fluid.
🚨Late / transmuralNecrosis signs
  • Pneumatosis intestinalis (gas in the bowel wall).
  • Portal / mesenteric venous gas (branching lucencies extending to the liver periphery).
  • Absent wall enhancement + free fluid; free air if perforated.
⚠️PitfallBenign mimics of pneumatosis / PV gas

Pneumatosis can be benign (cystic, in COPD/steroids/post-procedure). Correlate with clinical picture and look for the company it keeps — wall hypoenhancement, mesenteric edema, vascular occlusion. Pneumobilia (central, near the hilum) is not portal venous gas (peripheral).
