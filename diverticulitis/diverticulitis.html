Acute Diverticulitis on CT

Grade it with modified Hinchey, find the complications, and don't miss perforated cancer. Replace [bracketed] text.

⚠️ The decisions hinge on complicated vs uncomplicated: free air, abscess, fistula, and obstruction change management. And always ask — could this be a perforated colon cancer?

Diagnostic Findings

Core findings

🔍How to find itInflamed diverticula in the sigmoid
  • Colonic diverticula (most often sigmoid) with segmental wall thickening and disproportionate pericolic fat stranding — the stranding is usually greater than the wall thickening.
  • Engorged vasa recta, fascial thickening; sometimes a focal inflamed diverticulum with a fleck of gas.
  • Right-sided diverticulitis occurs (younger, Asian populations) and mimics appendicitis.
⚠️PitfallDiverticulitis vs perforated colon cancer
  • Favor cancer: short segment, shouldered abrupt margins, pericolic lymph nodes, luminal mass, little surrounding fat stranding relative to wall thickening.
  • Favor diverticulitis: longer segment, fat stranding out of proportion to wall thickening, fluid at the root of the mesentery, multiple diverticula.
  • Recommend interval colonoscopy after resolution to exclude malignancy.

Modified Hinchey grading

StageCT finding
0Mild clinical diverticulitis; wall thickening only
IaConfined pericolic inflammation / phlegmon
IbPericolic / mesocolic abscess
IIPelvic / distant intra-abdominal abscess
IIIPurulent peritonitis (free fluid, no enteric contrast leak)
IVFeculent peritonitis (free communication with bowel lumen)
🚨ComplicationsLook for all four
  • Abscess (rim-enhancing fluid ± gas) — size it; ≥ 3–4 cm often drained.
  • Perforation: extraluminal gas (focal locules vs large pneumoperitoneum).
  • Fistula (colovesical = gas in the bladder without instrumentation; colovaginal, coloenteric).
  • Obstruction from inflammatory stricture.

Uncomplicated sigmoid diverticulitis

F: Segmental wall thickening of the sigmoid colon with numerous diverticula and disproportionate pericolic fat stranding over a [X] cm segment. Engorged vasa recta. No abscess, extraluminal gas, fistula, or obstruction.

I: Acute uncomplicated sigmoid diverticulitis (modified Hinchey [Ia]).

Complicated diverticulitis with abscess

F: Sigmoid diverticulitis with a [X × Y] cm rim-enhancing pericolic fluid collection containing gas, consistent with abscess. [Adjacent locules of extraluminal gas.] No large-volume pneumoperitoneum.

I: Complicated sigmoid diverticulitis with a [X] cm pericolic abscess (modified Hinchey [Ib/II]). [Amenable to percutaneous drainage.] Communicated to [name] at [time].

Negative

F: Colonic diverticulosis without wall thickening, pericolic fat stranding, abscess, or free air.

I: Diverticulosis without CT evidence of acute diverticulitis.

Search pattern

  • 1. Identify the inflamed segment (usually sigmoid); measure length.
  • 2. Wall thickening vs fat stranding ratio; assess for mass/shouldering (cancer).
  • 3. Complications: abscess (size), extraluminal gas, fistula (bladder gas), obstruction.
  • 4. Assign modified Hinchey stage.
  • 5. Recommend interval colonoscopy if features are atypical.
