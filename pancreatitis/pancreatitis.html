Acute Pancreatitis on CT

Interstitial vs necrotizing, the revised Atlanta collections, and vascular complications. Replace [bracketed] text.

⚠️ Diagnosis is usually clinical + enzymes. CT is for complications and severity — and is most accurate for necrosis after ~72 h. Don't under-call early; don't over-call necrosis on a too-early scan.

Interstitial edematous pancreatitis

F: Diffuse enlargement of the pancreas with homogeneous parenchymal enhancement, peripancreatic fat stranding, and small acute peripancreatic fluid in the [anterior pararenal space]. No non-enhancing parenchyma to suggest necrosis. No organized collection, gas, or pseudoaneurysm.

I: Acute interstitial edematous pancreatitis without necrosis (CT severity index [X]/10). [Cholelithiasis — consider biliary etiology.]

Necrotizing pancreatitis

F: Non-enhancement of the [pancreatic body/tail, ~X%] consistent with parenchymal necrosis, with a heterogeneous acute necrotic collection in the lesser sac. [No gas within the collection.] [Splenic vein thrombosis.]

I: Necrotizing pancreatitis with [~X%] parenchymal necrosis and an acute necrotic collection (CT severity index [X]/10). [No imaging evidence of infection.]

Infected walled-off necrosis

F: Encapsulated heterogeneous collection in the [lesser sac] containing non-dependent gas locules, without prior drainage, consistent with infected walled-off necrosis. [Adjacent splenic artery pseudoaneurysm.]

I: Infected walled-off pancreatic necrosis. Communicated to [name] at [time]; recommend drainage.

Search pattern

  • 1. Gland enhancement — interstitial vs necrotizing (% non-enhancing). Note scan timing.
  • 2. Peripancreatic changes; characterize and name collections by Atlanta (time + content).
  • 3. Gas in a collection (infection); arterial pseudoaneurysm; venous thrombosis/varices.
  • 4. Etiology clues: gallstones/CBD dilatation, pancreatic mass.
  • 5. Assign CT severity index.

Type & Severity

Interstitial vs necrotizing

🔍How to find itLook at gland enhancement
  • Interstitial edematous: gland enhances (may be heterogeneous/edematous), peripancreatic stranding/fluid.
  • Necrotizing: non-enhancing pancreatic parenchyma and/or peripancreatic fat necrosis — best assessed after 72 hours.
📏ScoreCT severity index (modified, /10)
  • Inflammation: normal 0 · pancreatic abnormality 2 · peripancreatic changes 4.
  • Necrosis: none 0 · ≤30% 2 · >30% 4.
  • Extrapancreatic complication (effusion, ascites, vascular/GI): 2.
  • 0–2 mild · 4–6 moderate · 8–10 severe.

Collections (Revised Atlanta)

Name the collection correctly

TimeInterstitialNecrotizing
< 4 weeksAcute peripancreatic fluid collection (APFC) — homogeneous, no wallAcute necrotic collection (ANC) — heterogeneous, non-liquid debris
> 4 weeksPseudocyst — encapsulated, well-defined wall, fluid onlyWalled-off necrosis (WON) — encapsulated, contains necrotic debris
🚨ComplicationsCall these
  • Infected necrosis: gas within a collection (without intervention) — the key surgical/IR trigger.
  • Pseudoaneurysm (esp. splenic / gastroduodenal artery) — arterial blush within/adjacent to a collection; risk of catastrophic bleed.
  • Splenic / portal / SMV venous thrombosis; gastric varices.
  • Bowel involvement (colonic ischemia/fistula), biliary obstruction (gallstone cause).
